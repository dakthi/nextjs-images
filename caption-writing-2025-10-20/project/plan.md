# BlazingStar Gel Polish Shoot Plan

## Overview
- Goal: Campaign-ready lifestyle + application assets; in-house handles white-bg listings.
- Budget: ~£3,000 all-in (photographer+studio, editing cap, 3 models, nail tech, props).
- Deliverables: 25–30 retouched hero images + 40–60 light-edited selects + all RAWs.
- Date/Time: 1 shoot day (8 hours).

## Team
- Photographer: Lead + lighting
- Assistant: Product cleaning, prop handling, reflectors
- Nail technician: Application, cleanup, quick resets
- Producer/PM: Schedule, shot tracking, approvals
- Models: 3 hand models (varied skin tones), 1 color per hand

## Locations & Setups
- Setup A: White background (e-comm style) — softbox, pure white sweep
- Setup B: Lifestyle/self-care corner — warmer tone, lamp, towel, minimal props

## Shot List
### White Background Essentials
- Bottle solo (front, angled)
- Label close-up (shade name/logo legible)
- Brush detail (halfway out, viscosity visible)
- Gel taken out of bottle (macro)
- Drip shot (gel falling mid-air)
- Brush touching gel surface (ripple/meniscus)
- Trio: base + color + top
- Range lineup by concept/shade family

### Model & Application
- Model holding bottle gently
- Self-application (DIY angle) — brush on nail
- Close-up: brush on nail during stroke
- Finished manicure shine (both hands variant)
- Hand gestures (cup/book/phone)
- Before/after (optional quick pair)

### Lifestyle / Concept
- Self-care corner: lamp, file, towel, cuticle oil
- Bottles with props per concept (Romantic Nudes, Weekend Brights, etc.)
- Texture macros: gel swirl, brush stroke, pooled swatch
- Hero: bottle + model hand + color sample composition

## Schedule (8 Hours)
- 09:00–10:00 Setup and tests (lights, tether, props, product cleanup)
- 10:00–12:15 White-bg product set (all SKUs + details + drip)
- 12:15–12:45 Lunch / reset to lifestyle
- 12:45–15:45 Models (3x 60–70 min blocks overlapping with nail tech)
  - 20–30 min application (2 shades; one per hand)
  - 30–35 min shooting (application + finished + holding bottle)
  - 10 min buffer/transition
- 15:45–17:00 Lifestyle sets and hero frames
- 17:00–17:15 Wrap, backups, studio reset

## Model & Shade Map
- Model A (fair-neutral): Nudes/soft pinks
- Model B (medium-warm): Brights/corals
- Model C (deep-cool): Deep reds/plums
- One color per hand = 6 shades total featured

## Props Checklist
- Lamp (BlazingStar), nail file, buffer, cuticle oil, wipes, acetone
- Towels (neutral tones), mug, book/phone, small flowers/plant, fabrics
- Clear acrylic blocks/plinth for product elevation

## Gear & Technical
- Camera: 24–70mm + macro (90–105mm)
- Lighting: Large softbox key + reflector; lifestyle adds kicker or warmer fill
- Backdrops: White sweep; neutral textured surface for lifestyle
- Tether: Laptop with backup SSD; color-check card at start of each setup

## Editing & Delivery
- Editing cap: 6–8 hours @ £100/hr
- Final retouched: 25–30 images (skin, nails, labels, dust removal)
- Light edits: 40–60 exposure/white-balance balanced selects
- Formats: JPG (3000px long edge, sRGB), TIFF 300dpi for print
- Usage: Digital paid/organic, e‑comm, print up to A3, non-exclusive, 3 years
- Turnaround: 7–10 business days; 1 revision round included

## On-Set Checklist
- Clean bottles (no fingerprints), backup labels
- Shade order pre-approved and printed
- Shot list printout with checkboxes
- Snacks/water; lint roller; microfiber cloths
- Backup nails/swatches for texture shots

## Risks & Mitigations
- Time overruns: Lock two lighting setups max; prioritize hero list
- Reflections on bottles: Use larger diffusers/flags, polarizer if needed
- Shade accuracy: Use gray card; note any post tweaks needed per shade

## Sign-off Criteria
- All white-bg essentials captured
- 6 shades on hands across 3 models
- At least 3 lifestyle hero images
- Texture macro set complete
- Photographer confirms backups before wrap
