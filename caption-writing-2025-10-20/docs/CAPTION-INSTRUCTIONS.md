Preferred caption structure (use this format)
- Title line (H1): `# <Collection/Brand> ‚Äî Social Caption (<Edit/Theme>)`
- Hook line: 1-2 sentences that explains WHY this matters to the tech/technique ‚Äî how it solves a technical problem or improves the work. Keep it extremely short and punchy.
- Optional second line: Brand positioning statement or product-line context (e.g., "Build fast, build strong and build flexible with BlazingStar Builder Gels.")
- Two to three bullets (emoji-led), each one concrete feature/benefit/application. Prefer quality over quantity ‚Äî use 2 bullets if that's all you need.
- Required footer (three lines), appended verbatim.

**Hook line principles:**
- **Extremely short** ‚Äî aim for under 10-12 words for single line (can extend to 15 if connecting feature to specific benefit)
- **Address objections/assumptions directly** ‚Äî use pattern: "[Thing] doesn't mean [misconception]" or "You shouldn't have to choose between [X] and [Y]"
- **Connect features to benefits** ‚Äî "[Number/Feature] means [Benefit outcome]" (e.g., "32 shades mean 32 ways to create trending nail art")
- **Keep it concise** ‚Äî don't over-explain the benefit; trust the reader to understand (e.g., "32 ways to create nail art" not "32 ways to create nail art without hand-painting skills")
- **Add confident follow-up when appropriate** ‚Äî add "This is proof." or similar to strengthen claim
- **Can include brand/product line context** ‚Äî second line can reference broader product family (e.g., "Build fast, build strong and build flexible with BlazingStar Builder Gels.")
- Focus on technical advantage, not just features
- Answer: "Why does this make sense for the work I'm doing?"
- **Frame positively** ‚Äî focus on what you gain (efficiency, results) not just what you save (price, time)
- Use exclamation marks sparingly ‚Äî only for enthusiastic, fun products
- Match tone to product type (professional/serious vs trendy/fun)
- End with period, not question mark (statement of fact, not question)
- Can be 1-2 lines: objection/claim, then optional brand positioning or proof statement

**Content principles:**
- Remove redundant details ‚Äî if the hook mentions value, don't repeat "value pricing" in bullets
- Simpler language over technical jargon when possible
- Remove unnecessary qualifiers (e.g., "not harsh" ‚Äî just say "protective")
- 2-3 bullets is fine ‚Äî quality over quantity
